Developing and Testing Innovations (DTI): Enhancing STEM Engagement of Teachers and Underrepresented Students through Artificial Intelligence Driven Curriculum

Jackson State University (JSU) and Arkansas Tech University (ATU) are proposing a nine-month hybrid STEM training program for science instructors and high school students from historically underrepresented school districts in Mississippi based on using Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) along with the principles of Self-Determination Theory (SDT) to improve the underrepresented studentsâ€™ STEM learning experience and interest in STEM through training the instructors in creating a nurturing learning environment and developing AI- and ML-empowered curriculum. This proposal is to establish a mechanism for training URM students through our partnership with Jackson Public Schools Junior Reserve Officer Training Corps ( JPS JROTC) and the co-PI, Dr. Robin Ghosh. Dr. Ghosh has been collaborating with us for four years as the lead AI and ML instructor during the Summer Institute STEM program. As Dr. Ghosh has recently relocated to Arkansas and is in the process of establishing himself at ATU as an Assistant Professor, we are exclusively recruiting science instructors and students from Mississippi at this time. Our recruitment will later expand to include Arkansas."
